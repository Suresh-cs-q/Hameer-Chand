My research emphasizes advanced material synthesis and the optimization of capacitive deionization and forward osmosis processes to achieve efficient contaminant removal and resource recovery. Research

Performance and Mechanism of Chromium Removal Using Flow Electrode Capacitive Deionization (FCDI): Validation and Optimization (2024)

IF: 8.2 | Journal: Separation and Purification Technology | Article

Role: First Author

This study optimized FCDI with carbon nanotube-enhanced electrodes for Cr(VI) removal. Adding 1.5% CNTs boosted conductivity, achieving 99.5% removal at 0.9 V. High chloride levels did not hinder efficiency, and Cr(VI) was detoxified to Cr(III). The work highlights FCDI’s eco-friendly potential for heavy metal remediation via conductive, scalable electrode design.

Development of a Lanthanum-Based Metal–Organic Framework Electrode for Highly Selective Fluoride Removal by Capacitive Deionization (CDI): Performances and Mechanisms (2024)

IF: 13.4 | Journal: Chemical Engineering Journal | DOI

Role: Co-author

A carbonized LaBDC/C-NH2 electrode achieved 73.8 mg g⁻¹ fluoride adsorption (15× higher than AC) with 56.52 F⁻/Cl⁻ selectivity. Low energy use (8 kWh kg⁻¹) and stable performance over 10 cycles underscore its viability for industrial wastewater treatment. DFT/XPS revealed charge attraction and ion exchange as key removal mechanisms.

Mixed Veterinary Antibiotics Removal and Effects on Anaerobic Digestion of Animal Wastes: Current Practices and Future Perspectives (2024)

IF: 13.4 | Journal: Chemical Engineering Journal | DOI

Role: Co-author

This review analyzed mixed antibiotics’ (TCs, FQs, SAs) impact on anaerobic digestion (AD). MVAs reduced removal efficiency (~60%) and biogas yield, with TCs being most inhibitory. Inoculum content mitigated effects, though microbial impacts remain unclear. The work calls for deeper investigation into AD’s role in addressing antibiotic mixtures in waste.

Impacts of Draw Solutes on the Fate of Tetracycline in an Osmotic Membrane Bioreactor: Role of the Combination Between Membrane Fouling and Microorganisms (2023)

IF: 12.2 | Journal: Journal of Hazardous Materials | Article

Role: Co-author

OMBR retained >99% tetracycline via membrane rejection and fouling-layer biodegradation. Fouling enhanced hydrophilicity and hosted degrading microbes. Electrostatic repulsion (path coefficient: 0.655) dominated over microbial degradation (0.395). Findings emphasize fouling’s dual role in tetracycline removal, guiding OMBR optimization for pharmaceutical wastewater.

Deciphering of Microbial Community and Antibiotic Resistance Genes to the Selection Pressures of Antibiotic Concentration in Aerobic Activated Sludge Reactors (2023)

IF: 6.3 | Journal: Journal of Water Process Engineering | DOI

Role: First Author

Higher initial SDZ/TC-HCl concentrations reduced ARG abundance but diversified microbial communities, affecting biodegradation. Final antibiotic concentration correlated with ARG levels, driven by host bacteria proliferation. The study links antibiotic dosing, microbial dynamics, and ARG spread, aiding strategies to curb resistance in wastewater systems.

Shedding Light on the Transfer of Tetracycline in Forward Osmosis Through Experimental Investigation and Machine Learning Modeling (2023)

IF: 8.3 | Journal: Chemosphere | Article

Role: Co-author

FO tetracycline rejection was modeled via ANN, revealing convection, steric hindrance, and electrostatic interactions as key factors. Low draw pH and solute-specific effects enhanced retention. The model predicted transfer dynamics, offering insights for optimizing FO in pharmaceutical wastewater treatment.

New Insights into the Role of Molecular Structures on the Fate and Behavior of Antibiotics in an Osmotic Membrane Bioreactor (2022)

IF: 12.2 | Journal: Journal of Hazardous Materials | Article

Role: Co-author

OMBRs removed TC-HCl via adsorption/biodegradation (80–91%) and SDZ via membrane rejection (17–75%). TC-HCl’s π-π/hydrogen bonding enhanced adsorption, while SDZ removal relied on concentration-dependent electrostatic interactions. Degradation pathways reduced TC-HCl toxicity, informing OMBR design for antibiotic-laden wastewater.
